Russian influences on the American Identity


One of the Greatest influences on American Culture, is perhaps one of our good friends, and greatest enemies: Russia. Many of America's favorite Artists, Composers, and Creative Peoples come from Russia, and live and spread their influence and culture to the American people. Men like Rubenstein, Tchaikovsky, Balanchine, Stanislavsky, Mikhail Chekhov, and Joseph Brodsky all contributed greatly to what we consider as “America” and their names and Legacies will live on for countless years to come. Complex music like the music performed by Tchaikovsky and Rubenstein are played today by many popular musicians, and are still considered to be the finest in music. Russian Ballet, choreographed by Balanchine, hit top charts in America, and many Ballets are continued in the style that he introduced to america in the 1930’s. Stanislavsky was the man who introduced his characters in his broadway productions to “become the character”, to contrast the “cliched acting” at the time. A follower of Stanislavsky was Mikhail Chekhov, who was one of the best actors in America in the 1950’s.
He helped release a method for acting called the Stanislavsky Method which proved to be very effective when used in films since. Joseph Brodsky was a writer in the Soviet Union that believed that literature should encourage freedom of spirit. After years in a labor camp, he was forced to leave Russia, and began to release his works in America. Years of hard work allowed his works to reach the popularity of Steinbeck, and allowed him to share a little of his culture with Americans everywhere. Russia's impact on America is great, and Americans would never be the same without Russia. Russia might be our enemy, and our friend, but through all that, its peoples, and its influences on America as a whole could never be replaced.
